Blood Sugar Regulation

Maintains Blood Sugar in a approporiate range

Hormones involved

Insulin Decrease Blood Sugar, From Pancrea
Glucagon Increase Blood Sugar, From Pancrea
Epinephrine Increase Blood Sugar, Mobilize Carbohydrate Stores responding to stress, From Adrenal Medulla
Norepinephrine Increase Blood Sugar, Turn Glycogen to Glucose responding to stress, From Adrenal Medulla
Cortisol Increase Blood Sugar, Make sugar available for Metabolism, From Adrenal Cortex

Insulin

  • Source: beta-cells, Islets of Langerhans, Pancrea
  • Sink: Liver, muscle, other cells
  • Effect:
    • Body cell becomes more permeable to glucose
    • Liver converts Glucose to Glycogen
    • BloodSugar Decreases

    • Negative Feedback Loop

    Take in sugars -> Increase Blood Sugar -> Deteted by insulin secret cells -> Liver takes up Glucose -> Blood Sugar decreases and process stops.

Glucagon

  • Source: alpha-cells, Islets of Langerhans, Pancrea
  • Sink: Liver cells
  • Effect:
    • Liver converts Glycogen to Glucose
    • BloodSugar Increases

Diabetes:

  • Lack of production and using insulin
  • Glucose only goes into cell with Insulin, therefore the victims can't have glucose as a energy source.
    • Therefore Hyperglycemia (High blood sugar)
    • Body uses Fatty Acid as Energy Source
    • Kidney can't re-absorb excess sugar
  • The sugar eventually leaves the body with urine via osmosis
